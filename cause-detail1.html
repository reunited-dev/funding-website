The Football Club of Metz is a French professional football club created in 1919 in Metz in Moselle. The club is playing in Ligue 2 for the 2022-2023 season. The football section of the Cercle athlétique messin became autonomous in 1932 under the name of Football Club de Metz

OPPORTUNITIES

Metz, a new stadium and economic model All these improvements are part of the specifications for the renovation work undertaken by the club since 2019, alongside the construction of the imposing new stand which should provide the Saint-Symphorien stadium with a capacity of 30,000 seats. By becoming, in 2018, a year before its centenary, one of the rare French clubs to own its own enclosure, via a 50-year emphyteutic lease, FC Metz split its activities into two: the SASP for the athlete, the “matchday” and the FC Metz Stadium for real estate assets, excluding “matchday”.

GOALS

The stated objective in Ligue 2 this season is to find the elite. The conditions are special due to the transition to 18 clubs, the transition requires having only two clubs promoted this year. FC Metz during the first half of the season is in the middle of the table. Without neglecting the first entity, which must ensure its financial balance through traditional resources (TV rights, ticketing, transfers and marketing), it is towards the second that the margin for progress is the greatest. By exploiting the diversification of use of these new real estate assets linked to the stadium (premium tennis club, restaurants, modular spaces of 5,000 m2, business circle, coworking on 1,500 m2 of offices, premises intended for local and regional businesses, and "big event" section in connection with the SASP), for a cost of €80 million financed by the local authorities and the club, the leaders of Metz hope to enter a new dimension.
